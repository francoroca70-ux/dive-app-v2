Help me create a logo and full visual identity for my product, "Seven Seas."

## What Seven Seas is

Seven Seas is an operations platform for dive shops and boat charter operators — think of it as the internal software a shop's owner, managers, and crew use every day to run the business: trip and booking scheduling, digital waivers with signatures, boat and engine maintenance logs, gear inventory and equipment inspections, crew rostering, daily cash-up and financial reporting. It's not a consumer travel app and guests never see it directly — it's B2B software sold to shop owners and operators.

## Who buys it

Dive shop owners and managers, typically running small-to-mid-size operations (one to a handful of boats, a handful of staff). Many are ex-instructors or ex-industry people who've gone into business ownership rather than corporate software buyers, so the brand needs to feel credible and professional without feeling sterile or corporate-generic. It should read as "built by someone who's actually run dive trips," not "generic SaaS dashboard #4,000."

## About me (for tone/authenticity)

I'm a former dive instructor with several years of hands-on experience — Fiji 5-star resorts, Thailand, the BVI — including shop management and day-to-day commercial operations. I'm building Seven Seas myself because I lived the exact operational headaches it solves. That real, hands-on-the-boat credibility is worth reflecting in the brand voice: capable, direct, unglamorous-but-trustworthy — not a glossy vacation-brand aesthetic.

## What I need

A full visual identity, not just a logomark:
1. Primary logo (works as a horizontal lockup and as a standalone mark/icon)
2. A simplified icon version that reads clearly at very small sizes (browser favicon, mobile app icon, ~16-32px)
3. A monochrome/single-color variant that still works
4. Color palette with hex values
5. Typography pairing (heading + body) — needs to be readable in a dense, information-heavy operational UI, not just decorative
6. Brief usage guidance: how the mark behaves on light and dark backgrounds, minimum clear space, what not to do with it

## Where it needs to work

- The live product itself (currently a clean, functional web app UI — see current palette below)
- A browser favicon / mobile home-screen icon (needs to survive being tiny)
- A landing/marketing website
- Merch (t-shirts, stickers — via print-on-demand, dive/surf/freediving/nomad niches)
- Eventually printed materials a dive shop might hand a customer (so it should feel legitimate next to PADI/SSI-style industry branding, without imitating them)

## Current in-product design reference

- Deep navy 2: #0f2040
- Blue: #1a6b8a (primary accent)
- Teal: #4fc3f7 (secondary accent)

## Tone direction

Ocean/nautical, but please actively avoid the overused dive-shop clichés — no generic wave swooshes, no clipart anchors, no cartoon fish, nothing that looks like a resort logo. Think more "serious marine operations" than "vacation postcard": closer in spirit to how a well-run charter, a coast guard service, or a quality marine equipment brand would present itself — trustworthy, precise, a little rugged — while still being warm enough that a small shop owner feels it's for them, not for a Fortune 500 fleet.

## What I'd like from you

Start by proposing a few distinct creative directions (rough concepts/descriptions, not final art) so I can react and pick a direction before you go deep on any single one. Ask me anything you need about the name, symbolism, or business that isn't covered above.
