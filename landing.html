Why Seven Seas exists

Built by an instructor, not a software company

Before this was software, it was a stack of laminated checklists, a paper waiver box that got rained on, and a spreadsheet that only one person on staff really understood. I spent years as a dive instructor running day-to-day operations — Fiji, Thailand, the BVI — and every dive shop I worked in was solving the same problems with a different patchwork of clipboards, group chats, and half-used apps built for someone else's business.

Seven Seas is the tool I wish existed then: built around how a shop actually runs a day, not how a generic booking platform thinks it should.

Questions

Before you get started

The things operators usually ask before trying a new system.

Do I need a credit card to start?

No. The 14-day trial is free with no card required, so you can run real trips and see if it fits before deciding anything.

How do I pay after the trial?

Billing happens securely on the website by card — the same way most operations software works. It's not tied to an app store, so there's no extra markup and you're never locked into a store account to manage your subscription.

Does it work without wifi at the dock or on the boat?

Yes for the day-to-day tools — checklists, waivers, and gear/maintenance logs save on your device and sync automatically once you're back online. Booking and calendar changes need a live connection since staff share that data in real time.

Is it per staff member, or per boat?

Per boat, with unlimited staff on every plan. Add instructors, captains, or front desk without paying more per seat.

Is guest and waiver data secure?

Yes — data is encrypted in transit and stored with a hosted database provider, not on a shared spreadsheet or a shop laptop. Signed waivers are timestamped and exportable as PDF for your own records.

Can I use it on a phone or tablet?

It's built mobile-first — it runs in the browser on any phone or tablet, and can be installed to the home screen like an app for quick access at the dock.
